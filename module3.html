What's Making Oxygen Change?

Underneath everything you've seen so far, think of dissolved oxygen as a pool with three pipes attached, shown in the diagram below. Photosynthesis fills the pool whenever there's light. Respiration drains it around the clock, day and night alike. And the atmosphere connects to the pool from above, pushing oxygen in or pulling it out depending on which way the balance tips. Flip each process on and off below and watch both the diagram and the simulated oxygen curve react — it's the same light, wind, and temperature pattern from the real week you've been exploring.
