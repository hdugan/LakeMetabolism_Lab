Why is atmospheric exchange negative?

This lake starts the week well above equilibrium — supersaturated with oxygen, the way Module 2 described. Gas exchange always pushes oxygen toward equilibrium (the gray dashed line), never away from it. So when oxygen concentration is higher than equilibrium (supersaturated), gas exchange is pulling oxygen out of the lake and into the air.

That's what a negative number means here: the lake is losing oxygen to the atmosphere, not gaining. It's also why turning up your gas-exchange slider pulls your model curve down toward the gray line rather than up.
