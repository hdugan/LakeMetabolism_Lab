    Part 4 · What does NEP mean?

    An ecosystem's carbon balance, in one equation:

    NEP  =  GPP  −  ER

    Positive NEP: carbon entering storage exceeds carbon losses — the lake is accumulating organic carbon, like earning more than you spend.

    Negative NEP: respiration exceeds production — the lake is consuming stored or imported organic matter, like spending more than you earn.

    If many lakes have negative NEP, what does that imply about their role in Earth's carbon cycle? Many lakes receive large amounts of organic carbon from their watersheds. Microbes respire much of this carbon, producing carbon dioxide. As a result, many lakes emit CO₂ to the atmosphere — even lakes, like the eutrophic archetype above, that are among the most biologically productive ecosystems on Earth.

    Part 7 · Are lakes carbon sources or carbon sinks?

    Based on everything you've explored, cast your vote:

    Although individual lakes vary widely, many lakes around the world are net heterotrophic because they receive substantial organic carbon from surrounding forests, wetlands, and soils. Microbial respiration converts this imported carbon into CO₂, making many lakes net emitters of carbon to the atmosphere. At the same time, lakes also bury organic carbon in their sediments, making them important long-term carbon storage sites. Understanding the balance between these two processes — emission and burial — is one of the central questions in aquatic ecology.
